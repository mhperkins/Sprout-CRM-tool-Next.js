## Mission (canonical)

**Harnessing the power of the arts to end the loneliness epidemic in creative communities.**

Supporting line: *Sprout Society builds the spaces and networks where creatives belong.*

## Vision

A future where the creative life is connected, supported, and full of opportunity.

## One-line "what we are"

A Brooklyn nonprofit (flagship space in Bushwick) that builds the spaces and
networks where creatives belong, so no one builds a creative life alone.

---

## The problem (the framing to anchor "why")

- Roughly **half of US adults** report experiencing loneliness.
- The Surgeon General warns loneliness is as damaging to health as **smoking 15 cigarettes a day**.
- Independent creatives are **especially exposed**: the work is solitary, financially precarious, and lacks the infrastructure and stability most careers provide.
- The irony at the heart of it: the people who create connection for everyone else are too often left without it themselves. Artists are our connectors and storytellers; they need support and community to thrive.

## What we do (the four offerings — use these exact pillars)

1. **Community** — among artists who understand the work, in person and online.
2. **Dedicated physical space** — a reliable home base to work, collaborate, and share their craft.
3. **Economic opportunity** — networking and resources to pursue a vibrant creative life.
4. **Coaching & education** — to navigate an unmarked path.

## Programs (ways to connect, every day)

- Art, music & film from the community, performed and shown in the space
- Special-interest and hobby groups
- Daytime coworking for independent creatives
- Creative workshops and classes
- Peer-led support groups

## Founding story (Russell "Sprouts" Efros)

Sprout Society was founded in memory of **Russell J. Efros** ("Sprouts"), a
Brooklyn musician, artist, and filmmaker with a gift for making everyone around
him feel they belonged. He also struggled with loneliness. In 2018, at 25, he
died of fentanyl poisoning, in the middle of building the very thing Sprout is
today: a space to connect and showcase creatives. His family founded the
**Russell J. Efros Foundation** to carry his vision forward; that foundation
grew into Sprout Society.
