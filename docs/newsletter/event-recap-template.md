# Event Recap Template

Fill this out within a day or two of each event, while it's fresh. It feeds three things: the newsletter, an Instagram caption, and the event's own record in the CRM. The last field (**Newsletter blurb**) is the one that drops straight into the roundup template's recap block.

Keep it short. This should take 2 minutes, not 20.

---

## Recap form (copy this block per event)

**Event:** [name]
**Date:** [YYYY-MM-DD]
**Where:** [location]

**The hook** _(one sentence — what was the energy / why did it matter?)_
[e.g. "Our biggest Show n Tell yet — the room was packed with musicians, artists, and first-timers."]

**Highlights** _(2–4 bullets — specific moments, not a play-by-play)_
- [e.g. Live set from Tristan Normal Again]
- [e.g. Pat's DJ set with live vocals closed the night]
- [e.g. Art booths from Potters in Crime and Bob Greco]

**Shoutouts** _(performers, hosts, volunteers to thank by name)_
- [names]

**Photo(s):** [link or "see shared album" — pick 1 for the newsletter, 1–3 for IG]

**Headcount (internal only, not for newsletter):** [number]

---

### → Newsletter blurb _(2–3 sentences, paste-ready)_

This is the only part the reader sees. Warm, specific, no jargon. Write it last, pulling from the hook + one highlight.

> [e.g. "Our May Show n Tell was the biggest yet. The room filled up with live music — Tristan Normal Again, Foul Mistress, and a closing DJ set with live vocals from Pat — alongside art booths from local makers. Thanks to everyone who shared something."]

### → IG caption _(optional, looser)_

> [e.g. "What a night 🌱 Show n Tell brought out the best of Bushwick — live sets, art booths, and a whole lot of new faces. Tap in for the next one. 📅 Sprout n Tell, June 26."]

## Why these fields

- **Hook + blurb separated** so you brainstorm freely, then tighten into the 2–3 sentences readers actually get.
- **Shoutouts** double as your "who to thank" list and feed future spotlights.
- **Headcount flagged internal** — you said attendee numbers don't belong in the newsletter, so it lives here for your records only.
- **Photo as its own field** because a recap with one good photo outperforms a wall of text, and it's the thing most likely to get forgotten.
