# Member Spotlight Template

Fill this out when you want to feature someone in the roundup's Member Spotlight block. It feeds the newsletter and can double as an Instagram feature. Keep it short and warm: this is a thank-you, not a bio.

The last field (**Spotlight blurb**) is the one that drops straight into the roundup template.

---

## Spotlight form (copy this block)

**Who:** [name]
**CRM id (optional):** [ind_... — lets the agent auto-fill the name]
**Their thing:** [what they do / make / bring to Sprout — e.g. "DJ and producer", "ceramicist", "shows up to everything and makes newcomers feel at home"]

**Why them, why now** _(one sentence — what did they do recently that earned the spotlight?)_
[e.g. "Closed out May's Show n Tell with a DJ set and live vocals."]

**A human detail** _(optional — one specific, warm thing that makes them a person, not a credit line)_
[e.g. "Always the first to help newcomers find a seat."]

**Photo:** [link or "see shared album" — optional, the template has a photo slot you can delete]

---

### → Spotlight blurb _(2–3 sentences, paste-ready)_

This is what the reader sees. Warm, specific, grateful. Name what they do and why the community is better for them. No resume language.

> [e.g. "This month we're shouting out Pat Hopkins. Pat closed our May Show n Tell with a DJ set and live vocals, and is the first person to make a newcomer feel at home. Sprout is warmer because Pat keeps showing up."]

## Why these fields

- **Their thing + why-now separated** so you capture the standing role and the recent moment without blurring them.
- **Human detail** is what turns a credit line into a spotlight. Optional but it's the part readers remember.
- **Blurb written last** — brainstorm the parts, then tighten into the 2–3 sentences the reader gets.

> Spotlight candidates often come straight from event **Shoutouts** in the recap template. Thank someone twice and they've earned a spotlight.
