How to Make & Send a Newsletter

A plain-English walkthrough. No tech experience needed. If you can fill out a form and click a button, you can do this.

What you're about to do

You'll pick a layout, fill in a few boxes (the app even cleans up your writing for you), check how it looks, send a test to yourself, and then send it to your people. That's the whole thing.

1

Open the Newsletter page

It lives in the left-hand menu

Look down the dark menu on the left side of the screen and click Newsletter. This page is your home base: new layouts to start from sit on top, and anything you've saved before sits underneath.

1 2 3
  • 1Newsletter in the menu — click it to land here.
  • 2Templates — ready-made layouts. You'll start a newsletter from one of these.
  • 3Saved newsletters — every draft and sent issue you've made, so you can come back anytime.
2

Pick a template

Two layouts to choose from

Click the yellow Use this → button under whichever layout fits. Don't overthink it — most of the time you want the Monthly Roundup.

MOST COMMON

Monthly Roundup

Your full monthly update: a featured event with a photo, the coworking note, a membership ask, a community spotlight, and a list of upcoming & past events. Built to look great on a phone.

QUICK ONE-OFF

Quick Hit

A short, single-message send for between the monthly ones. One event, one update, one button. Use it when you just have one thing to say.

💡Nothing is final when you click Use this. It just opens an editor. You can change everything, save it for later, or throw it away.
3

Meet the editor

You type on the left, it appears on the right

This is where you'll spend your time. The left side is your worksheet — boxes you fill in. The right side is a live preview — the actual email, updating as you type. You never have to imagine how it'll look; you just watch it appear.

1 2 3
  • 1Your worksheet — the boxes you fill in (subject, your writing, photos).
  • 2Live preview — the real email. It changes the moment you type. This is exactly what people will receive.
  • 3Save buttons — top right. We'll cover these in Step 6. Your work is also saved automatically as you go.
4

Fill in the details

Subject line and the month

At the top of your worksheet is a small Details box. Two things matter here:

  • ASubject line — what people see in their inbox before they open it. Keep it short and inviting, like "June at Sprout 🌱".
  • BMonth label — like "June 2026." It shows at the top of the newsletter.

Leave Status on Draft for now and ignore Send date — we'll come back to those when it's time to send.

5

Write your sections

Where the magic buttons live

Below Details is the Sections list — one box per part of the newsletter (headline, intro, featured event, coworking, membership, spotlight, and your events). Just fill in the ones you have something for. Empty boxes simply don't show up in the email.

Two buttons make this easy, and they appear right next to the boxes:

  • ✨Polish — type messy notes however they come out of your head, then click ✨ Polish. The app rewrites it into clean, friendly Sprout-style sentences. You can polish more than once and pick the version you like. Don't like it? Discard it — your original stays put.
  • 🖼Add image — click it to upload a photo from your computer. For the featured photo you can even drag the picture to choose what part shows.
📅For Upcoming events and Past events, fill in the first one, then click ＋ Add another event for each extra. Remove any you don't need.
💡Polish needs something to work with. If you click it on an empty box, it'll just ask you to write a few notes first. Jot the rough idea down, then polish.
6

Save your work

So you can step away anytime

Up in the top-right corner you have two save buttons. You really can't lose your work here — it also saves on its own as you go.

  • 💾Save draft — saves but keeps you in the editor so you can keep working.
  • ✓Save & close — saves and takes you back to the main list. Your draft is waiting for you under Saved newsletters whenever you return.
🛟Even if you close the tab or walk away, the app remembers your draft. Come back later and pick up right where you left off.
7

The four stages of a newsletter

Draft → Pending → Approved → Sent

Every newsletter moves through four stages. You set the stage with the Status dropdown in the Details box. This is a safety net: the big Send to list button only switches on once you've marked it Approved, so nothing goes out by accident.

🔒You can't send to your whole list until the status says Approved. That's on purpose — it gives you a moment to be sure.
8

Always send a test to yourself first

See it in a real inbox before anyone else does

Find the Send box on your worksheet. Before sending to anyone else, send it to your own email so you can see exactly what it looks like when it lands.

1 2 3
  • 1Send passphrase — type the password Max gave you. This is required for any send, and keeps strangers from using it.
  • 2Test send — type your own email, click ✉️ Send test. It arrives with [TEST] in front of the subject. Open it on your phone and your computer to check it looks right.
  • 3Send to list — the real thing (next step). It's greyed out until the status is Approved.
9

Send it to your people

When you're happy with the test

Once the test looks great, you're ready for real. Three quick moves:

  • 1In Details, change Status to Approved. The 🚀 Send to list button now wakes up.
  • 2In the Send to list area, choose who gets it: All buckets, or just Community, Donors, or Prospects. It shows you how many people have an email on file.
  • 3Click 🚀 Send to list. It asks you to confirm the count first. Say yes, and it goes out from hello@sproutsociety.org. The newsletter flips to Sent automatically.
🚀This one is for real. Everyone in the bucket you picked will receive it. The test send is for checking; this button is the actual send.
10

Finding it again later

Nothing ever gets lost

Back on the main Newsletter page, scroll to Saved newsletters. Everything is sorted into Drafts, Pending, Approved, and Sent. Click any one to open it — to keep editing a draft, or just to look back at something you already sent.

That's it — you're a newsletter editor now 🌱

Pick a template, fill the boxes, polish your words, test it on yourself, approve, and send. Take it one box at a time and you genuinely can't break anything.
