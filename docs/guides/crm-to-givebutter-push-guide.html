Sending Your CRM Contacts Into Givebutter

You can push people from your CRM back into Givebutter so you can email them there. Here is what that means, in plain English.

The short answer: yes, you can

The other guide showed donations flowing into the CRM. This is the reverse trip: taking a group of people from the CRM and placing them into Givebutter, so Givebutter knows about them and you can send them an email blast.

First, one thing about "lists"

Givebutter groups people a little differently

Givebutter doesn't really have "lists" you drop people into. Instead, it groups people with tags (think of them as stickers). So the way this works is:

1

Decide who, and what to call them

The only two choices you make

Before anything moves, just answer two questions:

👥 Which group?

For example, "everyone in the Donors tab" or "everyone from the recent email-list import."

🏷️ What sticker (tag)?

The shared label they'll all get in Givebutter, like crm-donor. This is how you'll find them again to email them.

2

The contacts get pushed over

Done for you, behind the scenes

Each person in your chosen group is sent to Givebutter and given the sticker. Two helpful things happen automatically:

3

Email them inside Givebutter

This part stays in Givebutter

Once they're over there with their sticker, you head into Givebutter, choose that sticker as your audience, write your message, and send. The sending always happens in Givebutter itself.

✉️The actual "send" is a button you press in Givebutter, not something that happens automatically from the CRM. The push just gets the right people lined up and labeled for you.
★

Good to know before you do it

A few honest heads-ups
  • 1
    Some folks have no name, just an email. A big chunk of the imported email list has no first or last name. Givebutter usually wants a name, so we'd either fill in a placeholder or leave those few out. A quick decision to make first.
  • 2
    Your original donors are already in Givebutter. For them this is just adding a sticker. The truly new arrivals are the people from the email-list import.
  • 3
    Start with a tiny test. Push five people with a temporary sticker first, check they show up correctly in Givebutter, then run the whole group.
🧪Always test small first. Five people, a throwaway tag, confirm it looks right. Then do the rest with confidence.

That's the round trip 🔁

Donations flow into the CRM on their own. When you want to email people, you push a group back to Givebutter, give them a shared sticker, and send from there.
