Donations & Your CRM

Where the money comes in, where the people show up, and how the two stay in step. No tech knowledge needed.

The whole idea in one breath

You have two tools. People donate in one. Those same people show up as contacts in the other, already labeled as donors. You don't move anything by hand.

💜

Givebutter

The donation page. This is where someone actually gives money, buys a membership, or pays for a ticket.

→flows into
🌱

Your CRM

Your people list. Each donor appears in the Donors tab with what they gave and which campaign it was for.

Think of Givebutter as the cash register and the CRM as your address book. The register tells the address book who just gave.

1

Someone donates in Givebutter

Nothing for you to do here

When a person gives through any of Sprout's donation pages, such as a membership, a one-time gift, or an event, Givebutter records who they are and how much they gave. That's the starting point. You don't have to touch anything for this to happen.

💡Each donation page in Givebutter is called a campaign. The same person can give to more than one over time.
2

They appear in your Donors tab

Contacts → Donors

Open Contacts in the CRM. Across the top you'll see three tabs. Donors live in their own tab, separate from everyone else, so your giving relationships are never lost in the crowd.

3

Open a donor to see their giving

Click any name

Click a donor's name to open their full card. Alongside their notes and history you'll see their Campaign and a record of what they've given. This is the same information that lives in Givebutter, now sitting right next to everything else you know about them.

4

Tag a donor yourself (when you need to)

Add or Edit a contact

Most donors arrive already labeled. But if you ever add or edit a contact by hand, you can mark them as a donor and pick their campaign yourself. When you set a contact's Bucket to Donor, a Campaign picker appears. Just choose from the list.

5

Use it: thank, segment, and email

The payoff

Now that donors are sorted into their own tab with campaigns and giving totals attached, you can actually do things with that:

  • 1
    Thank the right people. Sort by Most given and reach out to your top supporters first.
  • 2
    Email just the donors. When you send a newsletter, you can aim it at the Donors group specifically, instead of everyone.
  • 3
    See the full person. Their giving sits next to their notes, events attended, and last conversation, so outreach feels personal, not transactional.
★

The one thing to remember

If you remember nothing else

Givebutter is where money comes in. The CRM is where you build the relationship. Donations flow one way, from Givebutter into your Donors tab, so the CRM is always your friendly, complete picture of who supports Sprout. You give in one place; you connect in the other.

That's the whole thing 🌱

You don't manage two systems. You let Givebutter handle the giving, and you use the CRM to know and care for the people behind it.

💜 Givebutter = the giving 🌱 CRM = the people 🎗 Campaigns tie them together
